Week 7 · Module 7

Emotional Resilience

Homesickness, code-switching, mental health, coping strategies, and the ongoing work of belonging somewhere new.

Learning Objectives

By the End of This Module, You Will Be Able To:

  1. Define imposter syndrome and code-switching, and describe how each can affect first-gen students in college.
  2. Identify at least three evidence-based coping strategies for stress, homesickness, or anxiety.
  3. Name at least one campus mental health resource and describe how to access it.
  4. Write a reflection that honestly names both a fear and a personal strength going into the first semester.

Learning Activity

Activity: Letter to Your Future Self

Instructions: Write a letter addressed to yourself at the end of your first college semester. In the letter, address:
  1. One thing you're scared of going into this semester
  2. One thing you're proud of or excited about
  3. One piece of advice from this course that you want to remember
  4. One question you hope you'll have answered by the end of the semester
This letter is for you. You will receive it back at the end of the semester [if you choose to share it via the form below].

Optional:
📬 Embed a time-delayed delivery form (e.g., FutureMe.org link or Google Form)
Assessment

Self-Care Plan: Coping Strategies + Campus Support

Instructions: Using what you learned in this module, create a personal self-care plan that includes:
  • Three coping strategies you'll use when you're stressed, homesick, or overwhelmed (be specific — "exercise" is less useful than "a 20-minute walk when I can't focus")
  • One campus support resource you've already looked up and know how to reach
  • One person in your life you can contact when things get hard
Also write the 400–600 word Module 7 Reflection Essay (Assignment 7): What are you most worried about going into your first semester — and what do you already have going for you that will help you handle it?

Example Reflection Essay — Proficient Level

✏️ Write a sample reflection essay from a fictional student.
