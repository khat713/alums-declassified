Week 6 · Module 6

Career Preparation

Resumes, LinkedIn, internships, networking without feeling awkward, and what employers actually look for in first-gen students.

Learning Objectives

By the End of This Module, You Will Be Able To:

  1. Draft a one-page resume that includes a header, education, experience, and skills section.
  2. Complete at least four sections of a LinkedIn profile (headline, About, Education, Experience).
  3. Write three distinct elevator pitches tailored to different employers or opportunities.
  4. Describe how to navigate a career fair and what to do before, during, and after.

Learning Activity

Activity: Three Elevator Pitches

Instructions: Write three short elevator pitches (30–60 seconds each when spoken aloud, roughly 75–100 words each in writing). Each pitch should be tailored to a different type of employer or opportunity:
  1. A pitch for a part-time campus job (e.g., library assistant, dining hall)
  2. A pitch for a career fair at a company in your field of interest
  3. A pitch for a campus organization you want to join
Each pitch should include: who you are, what you're studying, something specific that connects you to this opportunity, and one question to keep the conversation going.
Assessment

Career Document Draft: Resume or LinkedIn Profile

Instructions: Submit either a resume draft or a completed LinkedIn profile with at least 4 sections — whichever is more relevant to your goals right now. Also submit a 100–150 word note explaining one thing you're still unsure about and what your next step is.

Resume minimum: Header, Education, Experience (any paid/unpaid), one additional section (Skills, Volunteer, Activities).
LinkedIn minimum: Headline, About, Education, one Experience entry.

Submission:
📎 Upload resume PDF or paste LinkedIn URL + short note submission
