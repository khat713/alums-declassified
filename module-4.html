Week 4 · Module 4

Essential Life Skills

Grocery shopping, laundry, health insurance, leases, and managing adult life without a parent doing it for you.

Learning Objectives

By the End of This Module, You Will Be Able To:

  1. Create a weekly grocery list and meal plan within a $30 student budget.
  2. Identify the basic steps for doing laundry, including how to read care labels.
  3. Explain what a health insurance card is, how to use it, and what a copay means.
  4. Identify at least three key things to check before signing a lease.

Learning Activity

Activity: $30 Weekly Grocery List

Instructions: Using the meal planning guide from this module, plan 5 dinners for one person on a $30 weekly budget. For each dinner, list:
  1. The meal name
  2. The ingredients needed
  3. The estimated cost per serving
Then write out your full grocery list. Identify which items could be reused across multiple meals to stretch the budget.

Why this matters: A $30/week food budget is realistic for many students. Building this skill before you arrive is more useful than learning it hungry at 11pm on a Tuesday.
Assessment

Life Skills Checklist: What Can You Do?

Instructions: Work through the life skills checklist below. For each skill, mark whether you: ✅ Can do it confidently / ⚠️ Need to practice / ❓ Not sure how.

After completing the checklist, write a 300–500 word reflection (Assignment 4) answering:
  1. Which skills do you feel confident about, and why?
  2. Which are you uncertain about, and what makes them feel uncertain?
  3. Pick one uncertain skill. Describe specific steps to get better at it before your first semester.

Model Response

Example Reflection — Proficient Level

✏️ Complete the checklist and reflection yourself.

Write a sample reflection from a fictional student that addresses all three questions. Make it honest — "I have never done my own laundry" is a stronger opening than "I feel confident about most life skills." Show the level of specificity expected.
