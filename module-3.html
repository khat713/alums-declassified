Week 3 · Module 3

Financial Literacy

FAFSA, award letters, loans vs. grants, budgeting on a student income, banking basics, and scams to avoid.

Learning Objectives

By the End of This Module, You Will Be Able To:

  1. Read and interpret a financial aid award letter, distinguishing grants from loans.
  2. Build a monthly student budget using your school's actual cost of attendance.
  3. Identify at least two strategies for reducing expenses if your budget is negative.
  4. Recognize common financial scams targeting college students.

Learning Activity

Activity: FAFSA Section Walkthrough

Instructions: Walk through the FAFSA form using the guide provided in this module. For each section listed below, write one sentence explaining what information it asks for and why it matters:

  1. Student information
  2. School selection
  3. Dependency status
  4. Parent financial information (if dependent)
  5. Student financial information

Use StudentAid.gov as your reference. This is the official FAFSA resource — always free.

Assessment

Budget Worksheet: Build Your First Semester Budget

Instructions: Using your school's published cost of attendance and your own financial aid information (or estimates), build a monthly budget for your first semester. The template walks you through:
  • Fixed vs. variable expenses
  • All income sources (grants, loans, jobs, family)
  • Gap analysis — what happens if expenses exceed income?
  • Two concrete steps if your balance is negative
