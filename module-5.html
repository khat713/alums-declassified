Week 5 · Module 5

Academic Success

Note-taking strategies, study schedules, managing deadlines, professor communication, and beating imposter syndrome in the classroom.

Learning Objectives

By the End of This Module, You Will Be Able To:

  1. Apply at least two evidence-based study strategies (e.g., spaced practice, retrieval practice) to a realistic study schedule.
  2. Use the Cornell note-taking method to take notes from a provided lecture excerpt.
  3. Build a sustainable Week 1 college study schedule accounting for class time, study blocks, sleep, and personal commitments.
  4. Describe what imposter syndrome is and identify at least one strategy for managing it.

Assessment

Study Plan: Build Your Week 1 Schedule

Instructions: Build a realistic Week 1 study schedule for your first college semester using the provided template. Include:
  • Class times (use placeholders if registration isn't final)
  • At least two study blocks per day
  • Meals, sleep, and any work or family commitments
Also write a 150–250 word rationale explaining which study strategies from this module you built into your schedule and why they work.
